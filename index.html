Servo-Scribe — Playtester Guide

Servo-Scribe is a companion “ledger” for Warhammer 40,000 (11th edition). You move the models — on a physical table or in Tabletop Simulator — and Servo-Scribe keeps the bookkeeping (rosters, wounds, CP, VP, objectives) so an AI assistant can act as your opponent or coach. It works with any AI that reads text (and images, for board photos): Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and others.

The idea in one line

You are the hands and the board; the AI is the brain; Servo-Scribe is the shared memory that keeps them both honest.

1 · Build your armies

  1. Rename each army by clicking its title (“Your Army” / “AI Opponent”).
  2. Add units with + Add unit, or ⬥ Import list to paste a text export from the Warhammer 40,000 app, BattleScribe, or New Recruit. Import reads names, model counts, wargear and points automatically.
  3. List exports don’t include datasheet stats, so imported units show a ⚠ stats tag. Hit 🎯 Get stats from AI, paste the request into your AI, and type back the T / Sv / Inv / W / OC it returns.

2 · Play a turn (digital or tabletop)

  1. As you play, update each unit’s Models and Lead W (wounds), toggle flags (in cover, hidden, battle-shocked, engaged, reserve), set objective control, and adjust CP / VP and the round / phase at the top.
  2. Hit 📋 Copy state for AI. Paste it into your AI assistant.
  3. The AI plays the opponent’s turn (or advises yours): movements, targets, dice to roll, stratagems — then tells you what changed.
  4. Apply those changes on the board and in Servo-Scribe. Repeat.

3 · Real tabletop? Use a board photo

For physical games, the AI can’t see your table — so show it. Take a photo and send it to your AI together with the copied state (the state says what each unit is; the photo shows where it is).

  • Shoot from above, looking down the table — the single biggest factor in a readable photo.
  • Drop a tape measure or a d6 in frame so the AI has a sense of scale.
  • Label the clusters in your message (“five on the left ruin = Intercessors”), especially for unpainted or proxy models.
  • Keep objective markers and terrain visible.

What to expect: an AI reads positions, exposure, lanes and rough distances well, and gives strong strategic advice. It can’t measure exact inches from a photo or identify a mini’s datasheet on sight — so treat its measurements as estimates and tell it what each unit is.

4 · Saving & sharing

Servo-Scribe autosaves in this browser. Use ⬇ Export / ⬆ Import to back up a battle or move it between devices, and View / edit raw state to tweak anything by hand. Reset battle clears everything for a fresh game.

Button cheat-sheet

  • Copy state for AI — puts the whole battle on your clipboard as a ready-to-paste prompt.
  • Get stats from AI — asks any AI to fill datasheet stats for units missing them.
  • Import list — paste an army-builder export to create units in bulk.
  • swap (top) — flip the active player. Arrows step the battle round; tap a phase to set it.
